WHO WE ARE

We are a coalition of everyday people—organizers, humanitarians, doctors, artists, clergy, lawyers, and seafarers—who believe in human dignity and the power of nonviolent action.

In June, we launched a globally coordinated mobilization by land, sea, and air. In Summer, we return with a unified strategy: one goal and global coordination like never before.

Our efforts build on decades of Palestinian resistance and international solidarity. Though we belong to/hold different nations, faiths, and political beliefs, we are united by a single truth: the siege and genocide must end.

We are independent, international, and unaffiliated with any government or political party. Our allegiance is to justice, freedom, and the sanctity of human life.

STEERING COMMITTEE
Kleoniki Alexopoulou
Global Movement to Gaza (formerly Global March to Gaza), Greece

Kleoniki Alexopoulou is an economic and social historian specializing in the Global South. Over the past year, she has been a postdoctoral scholar at the Princeton Institute for International and Regional Studies. Her research focuses on colonial state formation, fiscal regimes, labour, and welfare development in peripheral contexts. Her work has been presented at international conferences and published in peer-reviewed journals and edited volumes.

Politically engaged from early on, she has been active in leftist and humanitarian initiatives, including UNHCR and SNV, and grassroots movements in the Netherlands. She is a founding member of a trade union and editorial collectives in Greece and has recently been involved in the pro-Palestinian solidarity movement across American university campuses.

Yasemin Acar
Freedom Flotilla Coalition, Turkey-Germany

Yasemin Acar is a dedicated human rights activist and organizer with a focus on social justice, refugee rights, and combating anti-Muslim racism. Over the years, she has collaborated with numerous organizations across Europe, and co-founded Berlin Arrival Support in response to the Ukrainian invasion, providing critical aid and support to displaced communities.

A long-standing advocate for Palestinian liberation, Yasemin is actively involved in the Palestine solidarity movement both nationally and internationally. She serves on the steering committee of the Freedom Flotilla Coalition and was part of the crew aboard the Madleen, sailing to break the illegal and inhumane siege on Gaza and deliver humanitarian aid.

Her work reflects a deep commitment to justice, dignity, and collective liberation across borders.

Thiago Ávila
Freedom Flotilla Coalition, Brazil

Thiago Ávila is a Steering Committee member of the Freedom Flotilla Coalition and was one of the coordinators onboard the Madleen mission that was intercepted and kidnapped by the Zionist entity in June 2025. He was put in solitary confinement after exposing Israeli State crimes in front of a Zionist judge and starting a hunger and thirst strike until his deportation.

He is a communicator and a socioenvironmentalist from Brazil who travels the world informing, educating, and mobilizing against exploitation, oppression, and the destruction of nature, especially in the Global South. He is 38 years old, the proud father of a 1-and-a-half-year-old daughter, and has dedicated himself to solidarity with Palestine for over twenty years.

Melanie Schweizer
Global Movement to Gaza, Germany

Melanie Schweizer is a political scientist, lawyer, and former civil servant who served as Senior Legal Advisor at the German Federal Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs. In early 2025, she was dismissed from her civil service role after publicly criticizing Israel over Gaza, and her dismissal sparked a national debate about free speech in Germany. She specializes in Business and Human Rights law and international public and private law. In June this year, she worked as a lead organizer on the Global March to Gaza.

Karen Moynihan
Global Movement to Gaza, Ireland

Karen Moynihan studied social science and international development and has spent over 20 years working in both paid and voluntary roles focused on social justice and solidarity. Her work has centered especially on unaccompanied children on the move in Europe, as well as with diverse communities across various countries and contexts. She brings extensive experience in grassroots organizing in Ireland and internationally, including her recent involvement in the Global March to Gaza in June of this year.

Maria Elena Delia
Global Movement to Gaza & Freedom Flotilla Coalition

Maria Elena Delia, from Turin, Italy, is a physicist, project manager, and teacher. She has volunteered in numerous countries around the world, contributing to projects focused on land development and child protection. Today, she is primarily dedicated to defending Palestinian rights. A founding member of the Free Gaza Movement and former Italian coordinator for the Freedom Flotilla Coalition, she has served since 2012 on the board of the Vittorio Arrigoni Foundation, which supports humanitarian initiatives in Italy and internationally.

Saif Abukeshek
Global Movement to Gaza, Palestine & Spain

Saif Abukeshek, a Palestinian activist based in Barcelona, has been organizing Palestinian solidarity across Europe for over 20 years. He was a lead organizer in the Global March to Gaza and currently chairs the Global Coalition Against the Occupation in Palestine and represents the Intersindical Alternativa de Catalunya (IAC). He also serves on the General Secretariat of the Popular Conference for Palestinians Abroad and sits on the board of the European Trade Union Network for Justice in Palestine. Saif is also a proud father of three young children, aged one, five, and seven.

Muhammad Nadir Al-Nuri
Cinta Gaza Malaysia

Muhammad Nadir Al-Nuri is a Malaysian humanitarian leader and founder of Cinta Gaza Malaysia (CGM), one of the country's most prominent NGOs supporting sustainable development and advocacy in Palestine. Since 2013, he has led initiatives such as the Malaysian Cultural Centre, Business Incubator, and School in Gaza, and has raised more than $20 million in aid since October 2023. A published author and influential media figure, Nadir has given over 500 talks nationwide and is widely recognized as a leading voice for Palestine in Malaysia with half a million followers across his social media platforms. He currently serves as Chairman of the MyAqsa Defenders coalition Supreme Council, and was recently a Goodwill Ambassador for the Aid4Palestine Coalition, a Special Envoy to the Middle East by Malaysia's Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Marouan Ben Guettaia
Jil Tarjih Leadership Academy

Algerian political activist, youth leadership trainer, and parliamentary consultant. He leads Jil Tarjih Leadership Academy and the Algerian Popular Coordination for the Support of Palestine, uniting civil society efforts for Palestinian solidarity.
He serves on the International Executive Office of IIFSO, a global youth organization with UN consultative status, and has a strong background in student activism and civic engagement. As a spokesperson and Maghreb Committee member of the Sumud Convoy, Marouan helps mobilize regional efforts to break the siege on Gaza and oppose normalization.

Wael Nawar
Sumud Convoy

Wael Nawar is a left-wing political activist and a lifelong advocate for justice and liberation. He served as Secretary-General of the General Union of Tunisian Students and co-founded the Coordination of Joint Action for Palestine. A former political prisoner, he was injured during the Tunisian revolution and has remained deeply engaged in grassroots mobilizations ever since. He also served as coordinator and spokesperson of the Sumud Convoy to break the siege on Gaza.

Hayfa Mansouri
Coordination of Joint Action for Palestine

Hayfa Mansouri is a Tunisian civil society activist and social researcher with a multidisciplinary academic background in engineering, philosophy, and sociology. Currently completing her studies in sociology, her research focuses on development policy, the role of NGOs, and grassroots social change in the Global South.

Over the past years, Hayfa has been actively involved in numerous political initiatives, student unions, and youth movements. She now works as a consultant and trainer with various national and international organizations, focusing on youth inclusion, civic engagement, and community resilience.

Deeply committed to justice and liberation struggles, Hayfa has been engaged in the defense of the Palestinian cause from an early age. She has played an active role in organizing boycott initiatives, awareness campaigns, and public events to support Palestine and challenge normalization. In 2017, she co-founded the Coordination of Joint Action for Palestine, a collective platform aiming to unify strategies and amplify the impact of solidarity efforts across civil society.

Torkia Chaibi
Million Rural Women and the Landless

Tunisian human rights and peasant movement leader, mother of two, and longtime activist for Palestine. She is President of Million Rural Women and the Landless, a La Via Campesina member organization mobilizing women farmers, fisherfolk, and landless workers across Tunisia.
Torkia serves on the La Via Campesina Arab and North Africa Secretariat and has twice been elected to the steering board of the Tunisian League for the Defense of Human Rights.

A committed voice for Palestine since 1985, she helped lead solidarity actions since October 7 and joined the Sumud Convoy with her son. She now represents MRWL and La Via Campesina on the Sumud Flotilla steering committee.
